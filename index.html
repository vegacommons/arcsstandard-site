Agentic Record Custody Standard

ARCS — Interaction Record Governance, Retention Architecture, and Surface Control

Version 1.0  |  Public Working Draft  |  March 2026

Abstract

Agentic AI systems generate records as a condition of operation: prompts, responses, planning traces, tool-call logs, memory updates, intermediate reasoning, and derived outputs. Existing standards address how agents communicate, what agents are permitted to do, and where execution occurs. None defines how the records produced by agentic execution are classified, assigned, propagated, retained, or discarded.

The Agentic Record Custody Standard (ARCS) defines a governance model for records produced by agentic systems. The standard specifies how records are classified, how custody is assigned across platform and vendor boundaries, how retention posture is declared, and how governance information propagates when records move between systems.

ARCS is vendor-neutral and implementation-agnostic. Any system may implement ARCS. No system is required to implement ARCS.

Scope

ARCS governs four aspects of record lifecycle:

  1. Classification. Records are assigned to defined classes that determine their governance posture.
  2. Custody assignment. Each record is associated with a custody surface identifying the system responsible for storage and policy enforcement.
  3. Retention posture. Each record carries a declared posture—ephemeral, session-bounded, governed-persistent, or durable.
  4. Propagation. When records move across systems, governance information travels with them.

ARCS does not define:

  • Agent communication protocols
  • Identity or authentication mechanisms
  • Behavioral guardrails or safety policies
  • Logging formats or storage implementations
  • Runtime architectures

The standard operates alongside those layers and defines the governance model for records produced within them.

Status

This document is a public working draft. It is published for review, comment, and early implementation. Subsequent versions will extend the specification to additional governance surfaces including discovery surface assessment, review surface disclosure, feedback routing controls, deletion and suppression controls, preservation and legal hold override, and additional operational controls.
